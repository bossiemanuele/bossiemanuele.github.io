## Motivation

Substrate-transferred epitaxial coating films are critical in applications where **extreme optical precision and minimal mechanical loss** are required. However, the transfer and bonding process introduces complex mechanical interactions that can lead to defects, stress concentrations, or incomplete adhesion.

This project aims to develop a **predictive modeling framework** for the bonding dynamics of these films, enabling manufacturers to better understand and control the transfer process.

## Application: Gravitational Wave Detectors

While the modeling framework has broad applicability across advanced manufacturing and optical systems, it is particularly relevant for mirrors used in the gravitational wave detectors.

In gravitational wave detectors, mirror coatings must exhibit:
- **Extremely low mechanical dissipation** (to reduce thermal noise)
- **High optical quality and uniformity**
- **Reliable bonding without defects or delamination**

Even microscopic imperfections in the coating or bonding interface can significantly degrade detector sensitivity. As a result, understanding and optimizing the **adhesion mechanics during film transfer** is a critical challenge in next-generation detector design.

This work is conducted within the broader research efforts associated with the LIGO collaboration through Prof. Gretarsson’s group.

## Technical Approach

The project focuses on modeling the **adhesion and deformation dynamics** of thin films during substrate transfer. The goal is to capture how physical parameters influence bonding quality and stability.

Key aspects include:
- Modeling **film-substrate interactions** during the transfer process
- Analyzing the role of **material properties** (e.g., stiffness, thickness)
- Studying **stress distribution and deformation** in the film
- Investigating conditions that lead to **successful bonding vs. failure modes** (e.g., partial adhesion, delamination)

A computational framework is being developed to simulate these dynamics and explore the parameter space relevant to manufacturing.

## Impact

The ultimate goal of this work is to provide **actionable guidance to manufacturers**, enabling:
- Improved reliability in film transfer processes
- Reduction of defects and bonding failures
- Enhanced performance of precision optical systems

Beyond gravitational wave detectors, the modeling approach can be applied to **advanced coatings, semiconductor fabrication, and high-performance optical components**.
