I am excited to share that our lecture proposal, **“Identifying Informative Physiological Indicators and Time Scales for Inferring Human Cognition During Automated Driving: An Information-Theoretic Approach,”** has been accepted to **ASPIRE 2026**, the HFES International Annual Meeting, which will be held October 19-23 in Reno, Nevada..

This work was completed during my research visit at Purdue University, where we explored how information-theoretic methods can uncover the most informative physiological signals and time scales for understanding human cognition in automated driving environments.

Two conference acceptances in the span of a single week is incredibly rewarding, and a strong testament to the quality and impact of the research carried out at Purdue. Again, I am sincerely grateful to my collaborator Sibi and and mentor Dr. Jain for their guidance and support throughout this research.
